CDC - Flu Symptoms & Complications
Influenza (Flu) can cause mild to severe illness, and at times can lead to death.
Flu helps is different from a cold. Flu usually comes on suddenly.
Common signs and symptoms of the flu include:
- Fever or feeling feverish/chills (not everyone with flu will have a fever)
- Cough
- Sore throat
- Runny or stuffy nose
- Muscle or body aches
- Headaches
- Fatigue (tiredness)
- Some people may have vomiting and diarrhea, though this is more common in children than adults.

Emergency Warning Signs of Flu Sickness
In adults:
- Difficulty breathing or shortness of breath
- Persistent pain or pressure in the chest or abdomen
- Persistent dizziness, confusion, inability to arouse
- Seizures
- Not urinating
- Severe muscle pain
- Severe weakness or unsteadiness
- Fever or cough that improve but then return or worsen
- Worsening of chronic medical conditions

People at High Risk:
- Adults 65 years and older
- People of any age with certain chronic medical conditions (such as asthma, diabetes, or heart disease)
- Pregnant women
- Children younger than 5 years

NHS - Fever in adults
High temperature (fever) in adults
A high temperature is usually considered to be 38C or above. This is sometimes called a fever.
Many things can cause a high temperature, but it's usually caused by your body fighting an infection.
check if you have a high temperature
You can check if you have a high temperature by:
- using a thermometer to take your temperature
- touching your chest or back – if they feel hotter than usual, you may have a high temperature
If you have a high temperature, you may also:
- feel shivery (chills)
- sweat
- feel hot and concerned
- have a headache
How to treat a high temperature yourself
You can usually treat a high temperature at home.
DO:
- get lots of rest
- drink lots of fluids (water or squash) to avoid dehydration – drink enough so your pee is light yellow and clear
- take paracetamol or ibuprofen if you feel uncomfortable
DON'T:
- do not put too many clothes or bedclothes on
- do not use a cold bath or sponge to cool down

Urgent advice: Call 111 or go to A&E if:
- you have a high temperature and a stiff neck
- you have a rash that does not fade when you press a glass against it
- you are bothered by light
- you have a fit (seizure)
- you have difficulty breathing
- you have chest pain
